Learn Quantum Computing

-By Akash Kumar Singh

I have been exploring quantum computing since my BS-MS days at IISER Tirupati and have amassed a wealth of resources throughout my journey. While I do not claim to have mastered everything, I am continually learning and progressing toward my goals. I believe in sharing knowledge and resources with others who are passionate about quantum computing and related subjects. Though to tell you the truth this is secretly my checklist that I want to complete some day. So below is a curated set of resources that I have compiled for those who wish to delve into this fascinating field. Before starting I must say that learning beforehand Linear Algebra, Ordinary and Partial differential equations can make your journey a lot easier though you can also learn it even after taking an introductory course of Quantum computing.
Though I am compiling list for broad area of Quantum computing but from now on I will try to be more focused on Quantum Algorithms and expand on that. 3. Classical and Quantum Algorithms.

To learn quantum algorithms at an introductory level, you may not need extensive knowledge of classical algorithms initially. However, to design quantum algorithms, understanding classical algorithms and complexity theory is essential. I have included Quantum Information notes by Prof. Scott Aaronson, a computer scientist, because traditionally, CS majors design algorithms. His perspective on Quantum Information Science as a whole is invaluable. In the future, designing algorithms will likely be the work of both CS majors and physics majors, especially those interested in quantum algorithms.

  1. Discrete Maths/ Maths for Computer Science (Proofs Based), MIT. Link
  2. Introduction to Algorithms, MIT Link
  3. Design and Analysis of Algorithms, MIT. Link
  4. Quantum Algorithms, Prof. Andrew Childs,UMD. Link
  5. Quantum Information 1, Prof. Scott Aaronson,UT Austin. Link
  6. Quantum Information 2, Prof. Scott Aaronson,UT Austin. Link
  7. Quantum Computational Complexity, Dr. John Watrous,IBM. Link
